## Heading rule (the load-bearing subtlety)
Corpus-slice drift guards do `text.index("## Step N:")` over skill_corpus() = SKILL.md +
sorted(references/*.md). SKILL.md concatenates FIRST, so if the spine carried `## Step N:`
headings, every slice would resolve to the spine's 1-3 line stub and the pinned content in
steps.md would fall outside the slice — silently un-pinning ~30 guards.
Therefore:
- The spine's per-step overview uses NON-heading syntax (a "Steps" section with `- Step N —
  <1-3 lines> (read references/steps.md §N before executing)` bullets). NO `## Step N:`
  headings in the spine, with ONE exception:
- `## Step 16:` STAYS in the spine (location pins in tests/test_wf2_clarity.py read
  SKILL.md directly: the Step-16 stub must contain `references/run-record.md` and
  `work_summary.py summarize`, must NOT contain `"workflow_version"`, and
  `<completion-gate>` must follow it in the spine).
- references/steps.md carries the verbatim `## Step N:` sections for ALL steps (1, 1b,
  2-16, incl. 8a) — slices then resolve into steps.md. Known exception: a corpus slice on
  `## Step 16:` would resolve to the SPINE stub (spine concatenates first) — acceptable
  because NO current test slices Step-16 content from the corpus (the two Step-16 pins read
  SKILL.md / references/run-record.md directly); steps.md §16 detail is reached by the
  orchestrator via the reading contract, not by any slice pin.
- AC2 deviation bookkeeping: no test permits or forbids where [Headless:] annotations live —
  the 33-count is corpus-wide — so keeping them with their step prose changes no test; the
  deviation is a documented judgment call (PR body + Decisions log), not a test edit.
