The honest part: the review waves paid rent.

Nine single-reviewer waves (~6 min each) caught a HIGH — --record doubling as the embed autonomy signal would have silently skipped #507's brand-new approval gate — plus three design-grade defects: #506's "complete" status unreachable on every live record, #508's WF16 number colliding with reserved #359 (shipped as WF19), and #513's supervisor timeout path recreating capture dirs at default umask. Cutting review means shipping those. The win is not stalling around the verdict and not re-running the full suite for prose tweaks — not reviewing less.
